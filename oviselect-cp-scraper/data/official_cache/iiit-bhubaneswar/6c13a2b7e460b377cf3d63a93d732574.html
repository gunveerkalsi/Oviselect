A short biography of the speaker:

Dr. Malaya Kumar Nath is Assistant Professor in Department of ECE of National Institute of Technology Puducherry. Dr. Malaya Kumar Nath received B.E. in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from BPUT Odisha and M.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering (Signal Processing) from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. He received his Ph.D. in Signal Processing from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. He joined the National Institute of Technology Puducherry, Karaikal, in July 2013, where he currently holds an Assistant Professor position in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering. He has more than 12 years of excellent experience as an instructor and researcher in medical signal processing, image processing, and machine/deep learning.

His research interests include the diverse fields of signal processing, image processing, and machine/deep learning with applications in healthcare. He has supervised three Ph.D., five M.Tech., and 24 B.Tech. scholars in signal processing. He has authored or co-authored over 57 technical papers published in peer-reviewed journals and conferences. These include 29 journal papers, 27 conference papers, and 02 book chapters. His publications have around 875 citations. h index of 18 and i10 index of 30. He has served on review boards for around 21 scientific journals.

He has delivered more than 27 talks in conferences, workshops, short-term courses, and institutes. He has organized numerous conferences and seminars, including AICTE ATAL FDP, GIAN, Digital India, and others.

Research & Development Cell
Vision: The Office of R & D Cell, IIIT Bhubaneswar will create an outstanding ecosystem for research and development that develops knowledge and contribute to the economic growth and societal development of the nation and benefit humanity as a whole.
Mission:
To enable the achievement of excellence by the research community, the Office of R & D Cell:
  • Develops and expands innovative research programs that align well with institutional mission and vision
  • Enhances and encourages faculty members and research scholars to obtain and manage grants
  • Supports strong infrastructure for interdisciplinary research
  • Promotes the ethical conduct of research, awareness, and compliance
  • Partners with the research community
  • Creates integrated services, transparent research administration, and streamlined processes
Expectations:
The institutional expectations from faculty and research scholars include the following:
i. Commitment to Research Quality:
  • Creating or associating with a formal research group/ lab/ research centre.
  • Documenting the details of projects, members, processes, activities and outcomes.
  • Scheduling the activities like periodic seminar talks, research meetings etc.

ii. Research Ethics:
  • Conduct of awareness meetings about research ethics involving but not limited to the following issues: plagiarism, research claims, managing human subjects, privacy protection, data security, ethics of online behavior, IPR etc.

iii. Resource Generation:
  • Proactively involve yourself in proposing research projects to research centres or external funding agencies.
  • Actively collaborate with other faculty members and external agencies to propose research agendas that push the state of the art, and/or addresses relevant social challenges.

iv. Social Outreach:
  • Proactively involve in making your research relevant to some stakeholders in the larger interest of the society.
