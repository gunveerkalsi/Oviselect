    Department of Computer Science and Engineering

    Introduction

    The Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at ABV-Indian Institute of Information Technology and Management (IIITM) Gwalior is dedicated to nurturing India's computing professionals for over two decades. Since its inception, the institute has offered various academic programs, with the undergraduate program in Computer Science and Engineering formally established in 2017. Following the restructuring of the institute into departments, the Department of Computer Science and Engineering was officially founded in August 2022. In addition to the undergraduate program, the department offers two postgraduate programs: (i) M. Tech in Computer Science and Engineering (going to start in July 2025) and (ii) M. Tech in Information and Cyber Security, catering to the growing demand for expertise in this critical area. Furthermore, an integrated dual-degree program (M.Tech. CSE + PhD) is going to start in July 2025.

    Students in the department are encouraged to undertake projects in diverse and cutting-edge domains, including Information Systems, Complex Systems, Distributed Systems, Data Mining, Computer Vision, Computer Networks, Cybersecurity and Software Engineering. The department also emphasizes emerging fields such as Cloud Computing, Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, Machine Learning, and Data Analytics, ensuring that students are well-prepared to address contemporary challenges in industry and academia.

    The department boasts a diverse faculty with expertise spanning theoretical foundations to practical systems, creating a balanced blend of young and experienced professionals. This rich mix of specializations enables the department to offer a comprehensive and dynamic learning environment. The department is actively developing state-of-the-art laboratories equipped with advanced tools and technologies to support its academic and research endeavors. These facilities are designed to meet the evolving demands of the curriculum and provide students with hands-on experience in their respective fields.

    With a strong focus on aligning with both industrial and academic requirements, the Department of Computer Science and Engineering is rapidly evolving into a hub of innovation and excellence. Its commitment to fostering industry-ready graduates has attracted some of the brightest minds, ensuring that students are well-versed in theoretical knowledge and equipped with practical skills to excel in their careers. The department continues to play a pivotal role in shaping the future of computing professionals in India.
    Vision and Mission
    Vision
    Global excellence in Computer Science and Engineering education and research as a valuable resource for industry and society adapting to the demands of the persistently changing digital world. 

    Mission
    • To provide in-depth knowledge through effective pedagogies and practical exposure using the latest tools and technologies in the discipline of computer science and engineering.
    • To advance academics by performing state-of-the-art research and providing industry-oriented undergraduate and postgraduate programs in the discipline of computer science and engineering.
    • To nourish and imbibe human values and excellence, equipping young minds to utilize their creativity and passion in serving the global community’s needs.

     

    Programmes offered by the Department
    • Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech.) in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)
      • A four-year undergraduate program covering core computer science subjects, industry-oriented electives, and hands-on projects. Admission is through JEE Mains, with an intake of 90 students.
    • Master of Technology (M.Tech.) in Information and Cybersecurity (ICS)
      • A two-year postgraduate program specializing in cybersecurity, cryptography, and digital forensics. Admission requires a valid GATE score and is conducted through CCMT, with an intake of 18 students.
    • Master of Technology (M.Tech.) in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)
      • A two-year advanced program in AI, machine learning, and distributed computing with a research focus. Admission is based on GATE qualification through CCMT, with an intake of 18 students.
    • Dual Degree (M.Tech. +Ph.D.) Program
    • A research-driven program combining M.Tech. and Ph.D., offering flexibility in interdisciplinary thrust areas, including Computational Intelligence, Cybersecurity and Forensics, Visual Computing, Theory and Systems for Data Computing, Communication Networking and IoT. The sanctioned intake is 5 students per year, with an early exit option for an M.Tech. degree, subject to the fulfillment of the required criteria. 
    • Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Computer Science and Engineering
    • A research-intensive program in the departmental thrust areas, including Computational Intelligence, Cybersecurity and Forensics, Visual Computing, Theory and Systems for Data Computing, Communication Networking and IoT. 
    • Post-Doctoral Fellow (PDF) Positions:
    • The Department offers two PDF positions per year in the five departmental thrust areas: Computational Intelligence, Cybersecurity and Forensics, Visual Computing, Theory and Systems for Data Computing, Communication Networking and IoT.

    Labs in the Department

    The following laboratories have been established in the department of Computer Science and Engineering.

    1. Lab Name: Secured Computing Lab

    Venue: Room No. 003, Block V

    Faculty In Charge: Prof. Shashikala Tapaswi

    Brief Description:The laboratory consists of servers and desktop computers. The primary purpose of the lab is to provide training, workshops on Ethical hacking, Network Security, Digital and Network Forensics. 

    2. Lab Name: Machine Vision Lab

    Venue: Room No. 114, Block V

    Faculty In Charge: Prof. Karm Veer Arya

    Brief Description:This research lab, in the Computer Science Engineering Department of the ABV-IIITM Gwalior, was established in 2015. This lab houses cutting-edge equipment for developing solutions to state-of-the-art research problems. This lab provides both hardware and software support to the researchers. It comprises high-end servers/workstations as computing facilities, machines for simulation, 24x7 power-backup, etc. For computer vision dataset generation, a specialized unit is developed in the lab that contains a high-quality camera and RGB colour background set-up with umbrella lights. The “Multimedia and Information Security Research Group” of the institute is also housed in this Laboratory. This research lab has contributed to 99 research articles, journals, and conference papers since its inception. PhD Research scholars and M.Tech. students work in this laboratory extensively to solve computer vision, bio-medical imaging, pattern recognition and artificial intelligence-based problems. Currently, researchers are working on problems like face super resolution, lung-nodule malignancy detection, multi-modal COVID-19 disease classification, crop disease detection, human emotion recognition, object detection, nature-inspired algorithms, etc. Many deep-learning models are proposed in the developmental phase of this research facility.

    3. Lab Name: Robotics Lab

    Venue: Room No. 213-B, Block V

    Faculty In Charge: Dr. W. Wilfred Godfrey

    Brief Description:Robotics lab is planned to expose the students to the general functioning of a robot. Students will be trained on a few general robots, and they will be allowed to implement their skills learned in other labs such as AI and control lab to design special purpose robots. Many B.Tech projects and M.Tech projects have been carried out in the lab. Further, two PhD students have earned their degrees working in this lab.

    4. Lab Name: Generic Computing Lab

    Venue: Room No. 005, Block IV

    Faculty In Charge: Dr Saumya Bhadauria

    Brief Description:The lab contains seven Acer i5 machines. The lab is mainly used for conducting lab classes for B.Tech and M.Tech students. Also, master’s and Ph.D. students use it as a seating place to carry out their research work.

    5. Lab Name: Machine Learning and Software Development Lab

    Venue: Room No. 002-B, Block V

    Faculty In Charge: Dr. Santosh Singh Rathore

    Brief Description:The lab consists of 5 desktop PCs (Intel i7, 16GB RAM), 1 GPU (Intel Xenon ® W-1290 CPU, 64GB RAM, 16GB dedicated GPU), 1 GPU (NVIDIA Quadro RTX 5000, 16 GB RAM). The lab is used by research scholars, MTech students, and project staff for research/thesis/project purposes.

    6. Lab Name: Multimedia and Information Security Lab

    Venue: Room No. 002-A, Block V

    Faculty In Charge: Dr Debanjan Sadhya

    Brief Description:The laboratory primarily caters to the academic requirements of the MTech first year students, specializing in the Information and Cyber Security program. Currently, the lab is equipped with ten desktop PC running the Linux Ubuntu OS with 2 GB RAM. This facility will be expanded later according to the requirements. The lab also serves as a sitting place for the MTech final year students during their thesis preparation.

    7. Lab Name: Computational Intelligence and Data Mining Lab

    Venue: Room No. 103, Block V

    Faculty In Charge: Prof. Pramod Kumar Singh

    Brief Description:The lab is currently used by one MTech. student and one Ph.D. student. Regarding computing resources, the lab is equipped with 12 PCs.
